# Fatturino — Design Context

## Users

**Tech-savvy Italian freelancers** (developers, designers, consultants) operating under Regime Forfettario (flat-tax regime, < €85k revenue). They're comfortable with digital tools and expect the polish they see in products like Linear or Vercel. They use Fatturino to manage FatturaPA-compliant invoices, track clients, and calculate taxes (imposta sostitutiva + INPS). Their context: they want efficiency and clarity — tax/invoicing is a necessary chore, not their passion. The tool should reduce cognitive load, not add to it.

## Brand Personality

**Friendly · Modern · Approachable**

The interface should feel like a well-crafted tool made by people who understand both design and the Italian tax system. Not cold or corporate — warm and inviting, but with the precision and attention to detail that tech-savvy users expect. Evoke confidence ("my taxes are handled correctly") without anxiety.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Clean and precise like Linear/Vercel, but warmer. The current warm cream + pastel mint palette is the foundation — it differentiates from cold monochrome fintech products while maintaining professional credibility.

**References:**
- **Linear** — Clean typography, precise spacing, monochrome base with strategic accent color, developer-focused polish
- **Vercel** — Generous whitespace, sharp hierarchy, minimal but purposeful motion

**Anti-references:**
- Cluttered accounting software (QuickBooks, legacy tools)
- Cold, sterile enterprise dashboards
- Overly playful/cartoon-ish fintech (too many illustrations, bouncy animations)

**Theme:** Light mode only (no dark mode planned). Warm neutral base with pastel mint accent.

## Design Principles

### 1. Precision Over Decoration
Every element earns its place. Prefer clear data hierarchy and sharp typography over decorative flourishes. When in doubt, remove rather than add. Numbers (monetary amounts, tax calculations) get special typographic treatment (JetBrains Mono) because precision matters in financial tools.

### 2. Warm Confidence
Financial tools often feel cold or anxiety-inducing. Fatturino counters this with warm cream backgrounds, soft pastel accents, and friendly (not corporate) copy. The deep forest green sidebar anchors trust; the mint accents keep things fresh and modern.

### 3. Effortless Flow
Tech-savvy users have zero patience for friction. Interactions should feel instant and obvious — subtle motion (150-300ms), immediate feedback, clear affordances. Progressive disclosure over information overload. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

### 4. Typographic Clarity
Inter for prose (with stylistic alternates for cleaner numerals), JetBrains Mono for amounts and data. Tight tracking on headings, generous line height on body text. Uppercase sparingly (table headers only). Font sizes create clear visual hierarchy without shouting.

### 5. Accessible by Default
WCAG AA compliance as baseline. Sufficient color contrast on all text, keyboard navigability, screen reader support. Pastel colors always paired with dark foreground text for readability. Focus states visible and consistent (mint ring).
